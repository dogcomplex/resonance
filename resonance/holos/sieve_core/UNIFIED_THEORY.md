## The Fundamental Equation

```
∂ψ/∂t = -i H(ψ) - γ ψ + S
```

Where:
- `ψ` = amplitude field over configurations (or distinctions)
- `H` = Hamiltonian (encodes rules as coupling)
- `γ` = damping (measurement/decoherence rate)
- `S` = sources (boundary conditions)

This is:
- **Schrödinger equation** when γ→0 (closed quantum system)
- **Master equation** for intermediate γ (open system)
- **Classical dynamics** when γ→∞ (instant measurement)

The interesting physics is in the **middle regime**.

## Key Theorems (Informal)

### 1. Emergence of Rules

**Claim**: Rules are not given. They emerge as stable meta-patterns.

**Argument**:
- Put potential rules in the same amplitude field as states
- Let them evolve together
- Rules that mediate actual transitions get reinforced
- Rules that don't, fade away
- The survivors ARE the laws of this universe

**Implication**: Physics is contingent, not necessary. Other rule-sets are possible.

### 2. Self-Annealing

**Claim**: The system finds its own temperature without external control.

**Argument**:
- Temperature = entropy of amplitude distribution
- High temperature = many patterns, similar amplitude (disorder)
- Low temperature = few patterns dominate (order)
- Damping causes temperature to decrease naturally
- Equilibrium temperature depends on rule structure

**Implication**: Solutions emerge without optimization. Just let physics happen.

### 3. Locality from Damping

**Claim**: Locality (finite interaction range) emerges from damping.

**Argument**:
- Amplitude spreads via rules (Hamiltonian coupling)
- But damping removes amplitude
- Net effect: amplitude can only travel finite distance before dying
- This distance IS the locality scale
- The "speed of light" = coupling / damping

**Implication**: There's no action at a distance because signals die before arriving.

### 4. Gravity from Complexity

**Claim**: Gravity is the tendency toward higher complexity.

**Argument**:
- Complexity = size of configuration space (entropy)
- High complexity regions = many microstates = more amplitude possible
- Amplitude flows toward regions that can hold more amplitude
- This is entropic force toward higher entropy
- = Verlinde's entropic gravity

**Implication**: Mass is localized complexity. Black holes are maximum complexity.

### 5. Dimension from Structure

**Claim**: The dimensionality of space emerges from rule structure.

**Argument**:
- Dimension = number of independent directions
- In configuration space: dimension ~ log(number of neighbors)
- The rule structure determines neighbor counts
- Different rules → different dimensions

**Implication**: 3+1 dimensions is not fundamental. It's an emergent property.
